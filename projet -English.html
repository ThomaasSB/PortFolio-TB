In February 2024, we explored data extraction from Google Maps for businesses using advanced web scraping techniques. We developed a robust and efficient Python script that leverages Selenium features and the Address API to automate the extraction process. This approach allowed us to accurately and efficiently extract relevant information such as geographic coordinates, opening hours, customer reviews, and other useful details from Google Maps pages. To handle the large volumes of data generated by this extraction, we implemented a partitioning strategy. This method allowed us to divide processing into smaller, manageable segments, optimizing performance and ensuring smooth execution even with large data files. Through this approach, we maximized processing efficiency while preserving the accuracy of collected data. Concurrently with script development, we deployed the solution into a production environment suitable for handling large data files. This step allowed us to test and validate our solution under real conditions, ensuring its reliability and operational efficiency. Finally, to report the results and insights derived from this data, we produced a detailed report. This report includes an in-depth analysis of the extracted information, relevant visualizations, and recommendations based on the findings. This approach helped to showcase the collected data and derive strategic conclusions for our clients.

In February 2024, our team focused on a detailed analysis of temporal data, specifically the evolution of solar energy production over the years. We developed custom R code using advanced techniques such as seasonality detection, trend identification, error estimation, and most importantly, forecasting future trends in solar production. By using forecasting models such as ARMA and Holt-Winters, we accurately anticipated expected changes in solar energy production. Our findings were consolidated in a detailed report, highlighting key insights, methodologies used, and solar production forecasts to guide investment and development strategies in renewable energy. This temporal analysis represents a significant contribution to understanding and planning the ongoing expansion of solar energy in the modern energy landscape.

During my first year, I acquired various skills that were put to the test during a significant project throughout my second semester, from February 2023 to June 2023. This project consisted of two parts: project management, where I acted as a team leader, and analysis, where I took on the role of an analyst. In project management, I led a team to develop a detailed report outlining our journey. I initiated a project schedule identifying key milestones for a realistic approach to meet our deadlines. Explaining our organizational choices and tools used, including methods like Waterfall and fixed-price delivery, was crucial. This aspect greatly enriched my project management skills and strengthened my leadership. As for statistical analysis, we received a database on state-guaranteed loans after the COVID health crisis. My initial task was to conduct descriptive analysis to identify trends and prepare our response to the problem statement. This involved data cleaning, excluding inconsistent information and unnecessary variables, followed by choosing appropriate graphical representations. We then conducted bivariate analyses to establish correlations between different data, including the distribution of loans by regions, departments, and sectors. I also performed linear regression to better understand the interactions between these variables. This project significantly enhanced my statistical analysis skills, covering various concepts and the use of software such as RStudio and SAS. Additionally, design components were emphasized, encouraging our creativity through tools like Canva or Photoshop. Rigor and organization were pillars throughout this project to meet the established deadlines.
